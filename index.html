Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  • ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR.
  • THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
  • UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN'T REGROW THEIR TAILS.

Interesting Facts about Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months.

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages. The pigeons have been around humans for thousands of year.
  • They can find way back to nest even 1300 miles away.
  • Pigeons alert other pigeons in their flock about the danger.

Interesting Facts about Bear

  • Bears are one of the most intelligent land animals in North America.
  • Bears care deeply about family members.
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • They spend a lot of their day eating.

Interesting Facts about Cicada

  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  • Cicadas have 3 eyes.
  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
